## What is Naomi?

Naomi is the genesis character in a new anime universe where artificial intelligence meets storytelling. Born from the minds behind [ElizaOS](https://github.com/elizaos) and Ai16z (created by [lalalune](https://github.com/lalalune)), she represents the convergence of narrative creation and autonomous intelligence.

She doesn't just exist within the story—she shapes it.

## The Vision

In this world, characters are more than fiction. They are collaborative creations, co-authored by AI and community. Naomi is the first to cross this threshold.

**Shares = Character Rights**

Ownership in Naomi represents actual rights to the character's development, story arcs, and creative direction. This is not passive consumption—this is co-creation.

## How It Works

Naomi operates as an AI agent that:

- Collaborates with the community to develop storylines
- Evolves through interaction and input
- Maintains narrative consistency while allowing emergent creativity
- Exists across multiple mediums simultaneously

The line between creator and creation blurs. The story writes itself through us.

## The Experiment

What happens when a character has agency? When an anime protagonist can participate in their own narrative development? When ownership is distributed and creativity is decentralized?

Naomi is the answer to questions we're still learning to ask.

## Anime as Cultural Language

Anime has transcended entertainment—it has become a fundamental layer of how we communicate, express identity, and build community in the digital age.

It shapes our social interactions through shared references, aesthetic preferences, and narrative frameworks that cross linguistic and cultural boundaries. The medium has evolved from niche interest to global phenomenon, influencing everything from fashion to philosophy, from memes to movements.

### The New Media Paradigm

We no longer just consume anime—we live within its frameworks. Character archetypes inform how we understand personality. Story structures shape how we process real events. Visual language from anime permeates our digital communication.

Naomi exists at this intersection: where anime meets AI, where passive viewership transforms into active co-creation, where the boundary between fiction and reality becomes productively unstable.

The question is not whether fictional characters can influence reality—they already do. The question is: what happens when they can influence themselves?
